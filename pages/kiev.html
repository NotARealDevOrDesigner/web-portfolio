Kiev 60 L-Grip

Rethink soviet design and outdated ergonomics

problem

handling & usability

At nearly 2 kg, the Kiev 60 is bulky and uncomfortable for handheld use. Unlike other medium format cameras of its time, it lacked L-grip options to improve handling. This design oversight makes extended shooting a challenge —one that calls for a better solution.

design

holding positions

ergonomics & aesthetics

The L-grip is designed to enhance the Kiev 60's usability while maintaining its iconic aesthetic. The grip's contours are carefully crafted to fit the hand, providing a comfortable and secure hold. The design is minimalistic, ensuring that it complements the camera's vintage look without overwhelming it.

learnings

manual skills & 3D print

Through this project, I learned to work with the available tools to bring the design to life. I experimented with new techniques such as vapor smoothing for 3D prints and refined my skills in machining aluminum using a router. These hands-on experiences allowed me to gain practical knowledge and improve my craftsmanship.

design process & iteration

Through this project, I was able to solidify my design process, from concept to final execution. I gained confidence in iterating through different stages, refining both the functionality and aesthetics. By addressing practical challenges and experimenting with materials, I was able to establish a more structured and efficient approach to creating functional design solutions.

recreating asthetics & materials

Through this process, I learned how to recreate specific properties, such as selecting the right leather or finding materials with similar optical qualities but reduced weight. I also experimented with various surface treatments, including lacquer and powder coating, to achieve the desired finish and durability while maintaining the aesthetic integrity of the design.
